This race determines whether Democrats maintain their razor-thin 19-18 majority in the Michigan State Senate. The district is a true toss-up: Kamala Harris carried it by just 0.44 points in 2024. Both sides are investing heavily — the DLCC has committed $250,000 to back Greene, while pro-Tunney forces including HOMESPAC, Americans for Prosperity-MI, and Michigan Freedom Network are active on digital. A GOP-linked group (ProgressiveMI) spent ~$118,000 during the primary to boost a weaker Democratic opponent, signaling the Republican ecosystem's strategic seriousness about this seat.

Current spending levels on digital are roughly even at ~$25.5K (pro-Tunney) vs. ~$22K (pro-Greene). However, the DLCC's $250K commitment — likely to flow into TV, mail, and field — represents a major resource advantage for Greene that must be matched or exceeded. The pro-Tunney coalition's willingness to invest is the key variable. A decisive TV and mail escalation in the final 8 weeks can tilt this race.

TV Market Assessment

The Flint-Saginaw-Bay City DMA (Market #66) is a cost-efficient market for TV advertising, typically 60-70% cheaper per GRP than Detroit. This is a significant advantage for pro-Tunney groups: a $300K-$500K TV buy here delivers strong market share of voice — whereas the same spend in Detroit would barely register. No confirmed broadcast or cable ad contracts have been filed yet for the general election as of this report date. This represents a first-mover opportunity to lock in premium inventory before rates escalate as the May 5 election approaches.

TV Buying Strategy Recommendation

Given the DLCC's $250K commitment (likely to include TV), pro-Tunney groups should move aggressively to secure broadcast time on WNEM (CBS) and WJRT (ABC), which deliver the strongest news audiences in the DMA. Cable insertions on Spectrum/Saginaw (1065) & Spectrum/Bay City (0125) allow precise geographic targeting within SD-35, avoiding waste on out-of-district viewers. Fox News, CNN, and MSNBC local insertions via Charter provide efficient reach to high-propensity voters. A balanced 60/40 cable-to-broadcast ratio maximizes both targeting precision and broad reach.

Critical Spending Gap

The DLCC's $250,000 commitment is the single largest known expenditure in this race and dwarfs all pro-Tunney spending combined (~$143K including the primary interference spend, which will not recur). In the general election specifically, confirmed pro-Tunney spending (~$25K digital) trails confirmed pro-Greene spending (~$322K including DLCC + digital + leadership transfers) by roughly 12:1. Pro-Tunney groups must close this gap immediately with a substantial TV, mail, and digital escalation.

Mail & Direct Contact

Known Mail Activity

Primary cycle: ProgressiveMI spent ~$118,000 on mailers boosting Pamela Pugh in the Democratic primary. These were "chaos agent" mailers designed to split the Dem vote — they labeled Pugh a "progressive champion" on Medicare-for-all and immigration. This spend will not recur in the general.

General election: No confirmed general election mail programs have been publicly documented yet. Pro-Tunney groups should anticipate 3-5 mail drops from the Democratic side over the final 6 weeks and plan a matching or exceeding program.

Strategic Assessment & Path to Victory

Current Position

Jason Tunney enters the general election as a viable but under-resourced candidate in a true toss-up district. His primary win was narrow (51.2%), suggesting the GOP base is not fully consolidated. His personal campaign has limited cash (~$48K COH), but outside support from HOMES PAC, AFP-MI, and Michigan Freedom Network provides a digital foundation. The critical deficit is against the DLCC's $250,000 commitment, which will fund a multi-channel campaign for Greene across TV, mail, digital, and field.

Greene has stronger institutional backing (DLCC, Senate leadership, organized labor), a compelling personal narrative (Marine veteran, firefighter), and higher name recognition from his decisive 60.4% primary win. However, his higher Meta CPM ($18.11 vs. the pro-Tunney average of $8.28) suggests less sophisticated digital operations, and a complaint about use of Saginaw Fire Department resources represents an exploitable vulnerability.

Probability Scenarios

Victory Path (Achievable with Coalition Escalation)

If pro-Tunney groups collectively invest $400K-$600K across TV, mail, and expanded digital over the next 8 weeks, Tunney can win. The Flint-Saginaw-Bay City DMA is cost-efficient enough that $300K in TV delivers dominant share-of-voice. Combined with 4-5 mail drops targeting persuadable voters and a scaled digital program, this level of investment can overcome the DLCC's spending advantage. Special election turnout dynamics (typically 15-25% of registered voters) mean that the side that dominates paid media can disproportionately shape the electorate. Estimated probability with full coalition investment: 50-55%.

Base Case (Current Trajectory)

Without significant spending escalation, Greene's institutional advantages — $250K DLCC backing, union GOTV infrastructure, and a compelling bio (Marine + firefighter) — give him the edge in a low-turnout special election. Current pro-Tunney spending of ~$25K digital cannot match the anticipated Democratic multi-channel blitz. Estimated probability on current trajectory: Greene favored 55-60%.

Bottom Line

This race is winnable. The district fundamentals are 50/50, the DMA is cost-efficient, and The pro-Tunney coalition has the resources to match or exceed the Democratic spending machine. The key is speed: the DLCC will activate TV and mail in the coming weeks, and whoever defines the narrative first has the advantage in a low-information special election. Pro-Tunney groups should begin TV buys by the week of March 16 and have the first mail drop in homes by March 23. Every day of delay is a day Greene's institutional advantages compound unopposed.
