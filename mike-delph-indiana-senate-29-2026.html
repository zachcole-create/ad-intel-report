Race Overview: Mike Delph, the former 13-year incumbent (2005-2018), is mounting a comeback bid for Indiana Senate District 29 after losing the seat to Democrat J.D. Ford in 2018. With Ford departing to run for U.S. House (CD-5), this open seat has drawn a competitive three-way Republican primary featuring Delph, former Sen. John Ruckelshaus, and school board member V. Roni Ford.

As of the 2025 annual campaign finance report, Delph enters the race at a significant cash disadvantage ($37,917 vs. Ruckelshaus' $151,984). No digital advertising activity has been detected for the Delph campaign on Meta platforms, and no 2026 broadcast TV filings exist on FCC records for any candidate in this race. The primary contest will likely intensify in March-April 2026 as the May 5 primary approaches.

TV Channel Strategy - Buyer Intelligence

Broadcast TV: Broadcast will reach more of your district, but carries a large waste factor. The Indianapolis DMA extends well beyond SD-29 boundaries (Hamilton County suburbs + portions of Marion County), meaning a significant share of impressions hit voters outside the district. Cost-effective for name ID but inefficient for persuasion at the state senate level.

Cable TV: Cable can be more targeted to SD-29 geography through zone-level buying. Choi's 2022 allocation (85% cable) validates this approach. Cable systems allow targeting specific zip codes and zones that align more closely with district boundaries, reducing waste and improving cost-per-voter-reached.

Fox News (Cable): Fox News is always important for a GOP primary. Local cable insertions on Fox News reach the most reliable Republican primary voters - older, engaged conservatives who vote in every primary. For a May 5 Republican primary, Fox News local spots should be a cornerstone of any cable TV strategy.

Strategic Assessment & Path Forward

Current Position

Delph enters the race as a well-known but currently under-resourced candidate. His 4:1 cash deficit relative to Ruckelshaus is concerning but not insurmountable given his existing grassroots network and finance team. However, the complete absence of any digital advertising or media presence this close to the primary is a strategic gap that needs immediate attention.

Scenario Analysis

40% Probability
Base Case: Ruckelshaus Wins
Ruckelshaus wins the primary. His financial advantage, Senate leadership connections, and moderate positioning give him the edge in a district that has trended purple. He can pivot most effectively to the general election against the Democratic nominee.
35% Probability
Optimistic Case for Delph
Delph leverages his grassroots network and social conservative base to win a three-way primary with a plurality. V. Roni Ford draws moderate/Pike Township votes away from Ruckelshaus, splitting the non-Delph vote. Delph activates his old base in Carmel/Zionsville where he has the deepest roots.
25% Probability
Victory Path for Delph
If Delph can close the fundraising gap quickly (Paul Shoopman team is capable of "ramping up to six figures quickly"), launch a targeted digital + mail campaign in March, and capitalize on Ruckelshaus' redistricting vulnerability with conservative primary voters, he has a viable path. MAGA network engagement against Ruckelshaus is key factor.

Strategic Recommendations for Delph

  1. IMMEDIATE (Next 30 days): Launch Digital Advertising Campaign Current Status: Ruckelshaus ran 25+ Meta ads in his 2020 race; Delph has zero. Even a modest $2,000-$3,000 digital buy can generate 100K+ impressions in the district. Focus on name ID reinforcement and issue framing (property taxes, public safety, experienced conservative leadership).
  2. FUNDRAISING SPRINT (March): Close the Cash Gap Target: Leverage Paul Shoopman's network to raise $100,000+ by April 1. This is achievable given Delph's prior fundraising history but requires immediate activation. Current $37,917 burn rate suggests ability to spend effectively if funds materialize.
  3. MESSAGE STRATEGY: Lead with Kitchen Table Issues Focus: Property taxes and public safety - the top issues in both Hamilton County and Marion County portions of the district. Avoid relitigating 2018 social issue battles that contributed to the loss. Frame as "experienced conservative leadership" vs. "establishment moderate."
  4. EXPLOIT RUCKELSHAUS VULNERABILITY: Redistricting Positioning Opportunity: Ruckelshaus' public opposition to redistricting is a real liability in a Republican primary. Without directly antagonizing Trump-aligned voters, position Delph as candidate most aligned with Republican primary voters' priorities. Test messaging with conservative media.
  5. V. RONI FORD DYNAMIC: Close Monitoring Scenario Planning: If Ford gains traction in Pike/Wayne Township areas, she benefits Delph by splitting moderate vote. If she fades, those voters consolidate behind Ruckelshaus. Delph team should monitor Ford's fundraising and grassroots activity weekly.
  6. TV ADVERTISING: Cable-First Strategy with Targeted Broadcast (Weeks 3-4 Before Primary) Recommendation: Follow the Choi 2022 playbook with a cable-heavy allocation. Prioritize Fox News local cable insertions - this is the single most important TV buy for a Republican primary. Fox News reaches the most reliable GOP primary voters (older, engaged conservatives). Supplement with targeted cable buys on additional news networks for broader reach within SD-29 zones. Budget a modest broadcast component on WISH-TV or WTHR for name ID reinforcement, but recognize that broadcast carries significant waste in the Indianapolis DMA for a state senate race. Suggested allocation: 70-80% cable ($30,000-$40,000) / 20-30% broadcast ($8,000-$15,000). Total TV investment of $40,000-$55,000 mirrors the Choi precedent ($50,103 in 2022) and could be decisive in a primary where TV advertising is uncommon.
