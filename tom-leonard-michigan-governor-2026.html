Strategic Recommendations

1

Never Go Dark on Advertising Again

Your five-month gap (Aug–Dec 2025) allowed competitors to dominate the digital landscape and eroded campaign momentum. Implement a continuous, year-round Meta advertising strategy with floor spend of $1,500–$2,000/month through primary.

Expected Impact: Sustained name recognition growth; prevents competitor narrative dominance

2

Aggressive Name Recognition Campaign

At 17% name ID with 200 days to primary, you need rapid awareness acceleration. Allocate 60% of remaining ad budget to awareness-focused digital (Meta, YouTube, programmatic display) and continued TV rotation. Emphasize "Tom Leonard, former House Speaker" in all messaging to leverage institutional credibility.

Expected Impact: 17% → 40%+ name recognition by April primary

3

Double Down on Utility Reform Issue Ownership

Your data center/electricity crisis messaging is unique and resonates broadly. Create 5–7 thematic ad variations exploring different angles: senior citizens on fixed income, small manufacturers, renters, home services. Build multimedia content library (TV, social, YouTube) around this differentiated platform.

Expected Impact: De-commoditizes primary race; attracts earned media coverage; populist appeal cuts across traditional demographics

4

Address Fundraising Trajectory Immediately

Declining quarterly fundraising ($646K → $91K) is unsustainable. Given cash constraints relative to Cox ($4.1M), Johnson ($9M), and James ($2.4M+ PAC), prioritize major donor cultivation, consider strategic self-investment ($200K–$500K range), and explore micro-fundraising digital campaigns to offset resource gap. Perry Johnson's entry validates self-funding viability.

Expected Impact: $1.5M+ cash position by March; enables sustained media through April primary

5

Leverage James' Debate Absence for Earned Media

John James' absence from both October and November debates and limited direct Meta presence creates opportunity. Continue strong debate participation, generate clips, and use social/digital to highlight his avoidance of public forums. Position yourself as the "serious candidate showing up." Pair with endorsement collection from debate-attending legislators.

Expected Impact: Narrative differentiation; credential-building among engaged primary voters; Mackinac activists already prefer you over James (23.5% vs. 14%)
