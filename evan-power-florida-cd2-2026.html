Redistricting Watch

Florida is embarking on a mid-decade redistricting effort that could significantly impact CD-2's composition. The Florida Supreme Court upheld Gov. DeSantis's 2022 map in July 2025, but the Trump administration has pressured GOP-led states to reevaluate their maps.

Historical Context: This district was represented by Democrat Gwen Graham as recently as 2015–2017. In 2022, redistricting forced incumbents Neal Dunn (R) and Al Lawson (D) into a head-to-head race.

Risk Assessment: New lines could shift the district's partisan lean in either direction. Your campaign should engage early in the redistricting process through RPOF contacts to protect favorable boundaries.

Strategic Recommendations

1

Launch Meta Advertising Immediately

You have a rare first-mover advantage — no competitor in this primary is spending on Meta. An initial investment of $5,000–$10,000 could establish digital dominance before anyone else enters the space. Recommended creative: endorsement highlight reels, RPOF achievement graphics (Trump's 13-pt victory), and "Conservative Fighter" brand video. Target Republican primary voters in CD-2 Panhandle and Big Bend counties.

2

Proactive FEC Complaint Response Strategy

The Gross FEC complaint creates a narrative risk. Rather than simply dismissing it, consider a counter-narrative ad campaign that highlights your record while positioning Gross as someone who "can't compete on ideas, so he files complaints." The RPOF rule clarification is a good institutional response — pair it with public-facing messaging that turns the complaint into a character contrast.

3

Localize Your Messaging for CD-2

Shift from statewide party-builder identity to district-focused advocate. Tallahassee connections are an asset, but Panama City (where both Gross and Rogers have roots) is the district's largest media market. Develop specific economic messaging around Panhandle priorities: military base preservation (Tyndall AFB recovery), tourism economy, hurricane resilience, and rural broadband. Your "every county" brand translates well to local engagement.

4

Aggressive Small-Dollar Fundraising Operation

With Gross pledging $5M+, you cannot match him dollar-for-dollar. Instead, build a grassroots fundraising machine that leverages your endorsement list. Each endorser should be featured in dedicated fundraising appeals. Your RPOF donor network is an untapped asset for congressional fundraising. Target $500K in Q1 2026 to demonstrate viability and earn media coverage.

5

Pursue Local CD-2 Endorsements

Your statewide endorsements are impressive but distant from CD-2 voters. Pursue endorsements from local officials: Bay County commissioners, Panama City mayor and council, Escambia/Santa Rosa/Okaloosa elected Republicans, Leon County GOP leadership, and local sheriffs. These counter the "outsider" narrative that could emerge from having Tallahassee as your base in a Panhandle-heavy district.
