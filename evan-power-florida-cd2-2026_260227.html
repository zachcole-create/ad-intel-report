Executive Summary

RPOF Chairman Evan Power filed Jan 14, 2026 to succeed retiring Rep. Neal Dunn in FL CD-2. The district covers the Panhandle and Big Bend (Panama City, Tallahassee) and is rated R+8 by Cook. Power positions himself as a "Conservative Fighter" aligned with the Trump America First agenda. He faces a 5-way primary; chief rival Keith Gross can self-fund up to $5M and has already launched an aggressive Meta ad campaign. Power has secured key congressional endorsements and the AG endorsement but trails significantly in digital ad presence.

Strategic Recommendations

1. Close the Digital Gap Immediately

Power's 2 Meta ads / ~20K impressions vs. Gross's 1.6M+ is an urgent deficit. Increase Meta ad budget to $2,000–$5,000/week minimum with 5–8 ad variations. Target Tallahassee and Panama City DMAs with endorsement-focused and Trump-alignment creative.

2. Leverage Endorsement Advantage

Power holds 5 congressional endorsements + AG Uthmeier — Gross has none publicly reported. Feature endorser testimonials in both digital and eventual TV creative. AG Uthmeier endorsement is the most valuable state-level asset.

3. Prepare for Self-Funding Onslaught

With Gross pledging $5M+, expect a massive TV/digital blitz in June–August. Power must build name ID and favorables NOW while ad costs are low. Consider early TV reservation in Tallahassee/Panama City markets for Q3.

4. Neutralize FEC Complaint

The Gross FEC complaint re: RPOF resources is a vulnerability. Ensure complete separation of RPOF and campaign operations. Prepare a rapid response brief. Consider proactive transparency measures.

5. Lock In Trump Alignment

In an R+8 district, the Trump endorsement or even a public nod would be decisive. Power's RPOF chairmanship gives him proximity. Priority should be securing any form of Trump recognition before Gross can claim the lane.
